To play, hit the play butto on main 

This is a two player digital version of Settlers of Catan built in Java. One player plays against a bot. The game follows standard Catan rules and is played by passing the computer between turns.
How to Run
Setup Phase

When the game starts you will see an intro crawl explaining the rules. Press any key to skip it. Each player takes turns placing two free settlements and two free roads on the board in snake order, meaning Player 1 goes, then Player 2 goes twice, then Player 1 goes again. During setup, click the vertex where you want your settlement, then click two connected vertices to place your road. After all four settlements and roads are placed the game begins. Player 2 is controlled by the bot and will play automatically.

Taking a Turn
Each turn follows this order. First click Roll Dice to roll. Resources are distributed automatically to all players with settlements or cities on the matching tiles. After rolling you can build, trade, or play a development card in any order before ending your turn. Click End Turn when you are done.

Building
To build a road, click Build Road then click two adjacent vertices on the board. Roads cost 1 Wood and 1 Brick and must connect to your existing road network or a settlement. To build a settlement, click Build Settlement then click a valid vertex. Settlements cost 1 Wood, 1 Brick, 1 Wool, and 1 Wheat, must be placed at the end of one of your roads, and must follow the distance rule meaning no two settlements can be directly adjacent. To upgrade a settlement to a city, click Build City then click one of your settlements. Cities cost 2 Wheat and 3 Ore and are worth 2 victory points instead of 1. cities are squares settlements are circles.

Development Cards
Click Buy Dev Card to draw a card from the deck for 1 Wool, 1 Wheat, and 1 Ore. You cannot play a card the same turn you bought it, and you can only play one card per turn. Click Play Dev Card to see your hand and choose a card. Knight cards let you move the robber and steal a resource, and count toward Largest Army. Road Building lets you place two free roads. Year of Plenty lets you take any two resources from the bank. Monopoly lets you take all of one resource type from the other player. Victory Point cards are revealed immediately and add 1 victory point.

The Robber
When a 7 is rolled, any player with more than 7 resource cards must discard half, rounded down. Then you must move the robber to any tile except the one it is currently on. After placing the robber you steal one random resource from any opponent with a building on that tile. The robber blocks resource production on its tile until it is moved again.

Trading with the Bank
Click Trade to open the bank trade dialog. Select a resource to give and a resource to receive. The rate is always 4 to 1.

Victory Points and Winning
In Catan, victory points (VP) are how you track progress toward winning. Every structure and bonus card you earn adds to your victory point total, shown in the sidebar next to your name. Settlements are worth 1 victory point. Cities are worth 2 victory points. The first player to build 5 or more connected roads gets the Longest Road card worth 2 victory points, and it transfers if another player builds a longer road. The first player to play 3 or more Knight cards gets the Largest Army card worth 2 victory points, and it transfers the same way. Victory Point development cards are worth 1 victory point each. The first player to reach 10 victory points wins. Throughout this readme and in the game itself, victory points are often shown as VP for short.

Privacy Between Turns
Since the game is played on a shared computer, when a turn ends a black screen appears for 3 seconds telling you to hand the device to the next player without looking. The sidebar hides the other player's resource counts during play so only the current player can see their own hand.

Building Costs Reference
Road, 1 Wood and 1 Brick. Settlement, 1 Wood, 1 Brick, 1 Wool, and 1 Wheat. City, 2 Wheat and 3 Ore. Development Card, 1 Wool, 1 Wheat, and 1 Ore. You can also click the ? button in the bottom bar at any time to see this reference in game.
